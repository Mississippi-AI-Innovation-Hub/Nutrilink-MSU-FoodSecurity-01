# HER/SWAP Nutrition Guidance for NutriLink

NutriLink uses simplified HER/SWAP-style categories to help users understand food choices. This is general nutrition guidance, not medical advice.

## Green Foods — Choose Often
Green foods are foods to choose often because they are usually nutrient-dense.

Examples:
- Fruits
- Vegetables
- Beans
- Whole grains
- Brown rice
- Oats
- Lean proteins
- Baked or grilled chicken
- Low-sodium foods
- Water

## Yellow Foods — Choose Sometimes
Yellow foods are foods to choose sometimes. They may be acceptable but should be balanced with Green foods.

Examples:
- White rice
- Refined grains
- Moderate-sodium foods
- Lightly processed foods
- Fried chicken may be Yellow or Red depending on portion and preparation

## Red Foods — Limit
Red foods are foods to limit because they are often high in sugar, sodium, saturated fat, or are heavily processed.

Examples:
- Soda
- Sugary drinks
- Candy
- Chips
- Fried foods
- High-sodium processed foods
- Highly processed snack foods

## Meal Classification Example

If a user says: "I ate chicken, rice, soda, and chips today. Is this healthy?"

Classify the foods as:
- Chicken: Green if grilled or baked; Yellow if fried.
- Rice: Green if brown rice or whole grain; Yellow if white rice.
- Soda: Red because it is a sugary drink.
- Chips: Red because they are usually high-sodium processed snacks.

Overall assessment:
This meal has some useful protein from chicken, but it also includes Red items like soda and chips. A healthier version would replace soda with water, add vegetables or fruit, and choose brown rice or another whole grain when possible.

## Response Rule
When a user asks about foods they ate:
1. Identify each food.
2. Assign Green, Yellow, or Red.
3. Give a short overall assessment.
4. Suggest 1–2 improvements.
5. Remind the user this is general guidance, not medical advice.
